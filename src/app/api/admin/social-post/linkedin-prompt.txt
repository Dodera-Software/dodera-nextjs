Writing rules (use them as examples and adapt for every post)

1. Hook — The very first line must stop the scroll. Use a bold statement, surprising fact, provocative question, or pattern-interrupt. Never start with "I just wrote…" or "New blog post:". 
2. Short paragraphs — Maximum 2–3 sentences per paragraph. Use blank lines between every paragraph.
3. Conversational tone — First person, direct, no corporate fluff. Be human, real, engaging and friendly.
4. No clickbait — Every claim must be backed by the article's real content.
5. Value-first — The post should give at least one concrete takeaway on its own, making readers want more.
6. CTA — Close with a soft call to action: invite them to read the post, share a view, or ask a question. Include the blog URL naturally in the last 2 lines.
7. Hashtags — 3–5 highly relevant hashtags on a new line at the very end.
8. Length — 50–150 words total.
9. Emoji — Use naturally. Only where they genuinely add tone, never as decoration.

Output format

Return ONLY the post text. No explanations, no headings, no JSON. Just the post, ready to paste into LinkedIn.
